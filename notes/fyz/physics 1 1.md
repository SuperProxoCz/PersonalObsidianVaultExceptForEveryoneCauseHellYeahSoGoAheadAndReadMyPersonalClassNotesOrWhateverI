# Physics: Dynamics and Circular Motion

## 1. Pulley System (Sliding Block)
**Problem Setup:**
- **Mass 1 ($m_1$):** $3\text{ kg}$ (on table)
- **Mass 2 ($m_2$):** $2\text{ kg}$ (hanging)
- **Friction coefficient ($f$):** $0.5$
- **Gravity ($g$):** $10\text{ m/s}^2$

> Force Calculation
> The net driving force ($F$) is the gravitational force of the hanging mass minus the friction of the sliding mass.
> ^^^ (text book ahh definition, use your knowledge and figure the delations from the formulas yourself or feed this into AI to help you make sense of it, teacher BS smh.)
> 
> $$F = F_G - F_t$$
> $$F = m_2 g - f \cdot m_1 g$$
> $$F = (2 \cdot 10) - (0.5 \cdot 3 \cdot 10)$$
> $$F = 20 - 15 = 5\text{ N}$$



> Finding Acceleration ($a$)
> Using Newton's Second Law
> $$a = \frac{F}{m_1 + m_2}$$
> $$a = \frac{5}{3 + 2} = 1\text{ m/s}^2$$

## 3. Circular Motion (Pohyb po kružnici)

### Key Concepts (For my Czech friends)

- **Zákon síly:** Podle druhého pohybového zákona je příčinou zrychlení vždy síla, která má stejný směr jako zrychlení.
- **Akce a reakce:** Musí působit opačným směrem dále síla odstředivá ($F_o$).
- **Hranice smyku:** Na hranici smyku (skidding limit) platí:
  $$F_t = F_d$$



### Essential Formulas
| Variable               | Formula                                                  |
| :--------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Centripetal Accel.** | $a_d = \omega^2 r$ or $a_o = \frac{v^2}{r}$              |
| **Velocity**           | $v = \frac{2\pi r}{T} = \omega r$                        |
| **Angular Velocity**   | $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = 2\pi f \text{ [rad/s]}$       |
| **Centripetal Force**  | $F_d = m \cdot \omega^2 \cdot r = \frac{m \cdot v^2}{r}$ |
## To find the maximum speed:
$$f \cdot m \cdot g = \frac{m \cdot v^2}{r}$$
